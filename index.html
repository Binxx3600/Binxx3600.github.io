intro

I have always been driven by curiosity, exploring the world around me and constantly seeking to understand how things work. From a young age, I was fascinated by the mechanics of everyday objects, often dismantling things to see how they operated. This early curiosity laid the foundation for my passion for innovation and problem-solving. I excelled in school from an early age, earning high marks and consistently impressing my teachers, who often encouraged me to pursue engineering. At the age of four, I began playing piano, eventually completing Level 10 RCM and advanced theory. My diverse interests grew to include basketball, athletic training, weightlifting, coding, competitive gaming, and automation projects. I graduated as valedictorian with a 98.33% grade average, and my academic success set the stage for my current studies at the University of Waterloo, where I am working towards a BASc in Mechatronics Engineering. Over the years, I have completed numerous automation projects that combine coding, robotics, electrical, and mechanical components, drawn from personal projects, jobs, and co-op experiences. Driven by an insatiable curiosity and a desire to make a positive impact, I continue to expand my skills and knowledge, striving to better myself and contribute to the world around me.
